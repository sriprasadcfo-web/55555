CBRNE Classification

CBRNE stands for Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear, and Explosives. Even though Sri Lanka lacks nuclear weapons, the misuse of these materials in industrial, health, mining, and research sectors poses a severe threat to national security.

C

Chemical

Toxic gases and industrial chemicals (e.g., Sarin, Mustard Gas, Cyanide).

B

Biological

Pathogenic bacteria and viruses (e.g., Anthrax, Ebola).

R/N

Radiological & Nuclear

Radioactive emitting sources (e.g., Cobalt-60) and nuclear fallout.

E

Explosives

Commercial or military explosives (C4, TNT, Ammonium Nitrate).

Chemical Weapons Convention (CWC) Schedules

Classification schedules dictated by the Organization for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW) to control and monitor hazardous chemical facilities globally.

Schedule 01

Highly toxic chemicals used strictly as chemical weapons (Production banned).

Schedule 02

Critical Precursors essential for chemical weapons production (e.g., Amiton, Thiodiglycol).

Schedule 03

Dual-use chemicals widely used in commercial industries like plastics and pharmaceuticals (e.g., Phosgene, Hydrogen Cyanide).

Legal & Institutional Framework

Sri Lanka has been a CWC member state since 1994, enforced via Chemical Weapons Convention Act No. 58 of 2007.

Institution Key Responsibility
Ministry of Defence Strategic oversight and policy making.
NACWC (National Authority for the Implementation of Chemical Weapons Convention) Regulating imports, usage, and issuing licenses for chemicals.
CEA (Central Environmental Authority) Waste management control and issuing EPL licenses.
SLAF (Sri Lanka Air Force) Detection of illegal radioactive material transit via airports.
14 CBRNE Regiment Army Specialized response unit during a chemical or explosive threat.

Radiation Protection & ALARA

The Sri Lanka Atomic Energy Regulatory Council (AERC) holds full authority over radioactive materials control. Radiation protection is governed by the ALARA principle: As Low As Reasonably Achievable.

01

Time

Minimize time near radiation source.

02

Distance

Maintain maximum distance from the source.

03

Shielding

Use dense shielding like Lead or thick concrete.

CBRNE Emergency First-Response Actions

1

Evacuate Promptly

Leave the hazard area immediately. Run upwind to avoid airborne gas, plumes, or particulates.

2

Decontaminate Skin

Wash skin and hair thoroughly with warm water and mild soap if exposed to radiological or chemical elements.

3

Isolate Clothing

Remove contaminated outer clothing and seal them inside plastic bags to prevent secondary hazard spreads.

4

Alert Authorities

Immediately contact AERC or NACWC regulatory response lines (+94 11 2 133 333) and the police emergency hotline (119).
